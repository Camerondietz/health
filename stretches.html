Pillar 02b · Mobility

Stretches that keep you moving well.

A small daily stretching habit pays back tenfold: better posture, fewer tweaks, easier mornings, and recovery that sticks. Browse the library, filter by area or time of day, or follow a ready-made set.

Ready-Made Routines

Pick a set and press play.

Three to seven stretches, sequenced. The morning set wakes the body up; the others target specific moments in your day.

Morning · 5 min

Good Morning Stretch

A gentle wake-up sequence that lubricates the spine, opens the hips, and gets blood moving — do it next to the bed before your first coffee.

  1. Cat–Cow — 8 slow rounds to wake the spine.
  2. Child's Pose — 30s to release the low back.
  3. Cobra — 20s × 2 to reverse a night of curling forward.
  4. Standing Side Bend — 20s each side to open the ribs.
  5. Standing Forward Fold (hamstring fold) — 30s to lengthen the posterior chain.
  6. Hip Flexor Lunge — 30s each side to undo sleep's hip flexion.
  7. Neck Side Stretch — 20s each side to finish.

Total: ~5 minutes · No equipment · Filter by Morning below to see each move.

Desk · 3 min

Desk Reset

Counters hours of typing and screen-staring. Do once mid-morning and once mid-afternoon.

  1. Neck Side Stretch — 20s each side.
  2. Shoulder Rolls + Across-Body — 30s.
  3. Doorway Chest Opener — 30s.
  4. Wrist & Forearm — 20s each side.
  5. Seated Spinal Twist — 20s each side.

Total: ~3 minutes · Do at your desk.

Post-Workout · 6 min

Post-Workout Cooldown

Static holds after training, while muscles are warm — the time most research suggests stretching actually improves range.

  1. Standing Quad Stretch — 30s each side.
  2. Hamstring Forward Fold — 45s.
  3. Calf Wall Stretch — 30s each side.
  4. Hip Flexor Lunge — 30s each side.
  5. Pigeon Pose — 45s each side.
  6. Child's Pose — 45s to finish.

Total: ~6 minutes · After strength or cardio.

Bedtime · 4 min

Bedtime Wind-Down

Slow, floor-based holds that nudge the nervous system toward rest. Pairs well with the sleep guide.

  1. Child's Pose — 60s, breathe slowly.
  2. Figure-4 Glute Stretch — 45s each side, lying down.
  3. Knees-to-Chest — 30s.
  4. Seated Spinal Twist — 30s each side.

Total: ~4 minutes · On the bedroom floor or in bed.
